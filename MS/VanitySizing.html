Vanity Sizing: It's Affects on the Fashion Industry, Economy and Human Physcology

What Is Vanity Sizing?

Also called size inflation, "vanity sizing is the practice of labeling clothing with sizes smaller than its actual measurements, aiming to make customers feel like they are wearing a smaller size and potentially boost sales."

Example: A size 8 in the 1950s would be a 00 today.

Vanity sizing affects people's fashion spending, psycological body image, and what businesses they shop at. Business with vanity sizings are more likely to sell clothes to customers. This is because customers feel more petite or thin in smaller number sizes. The industry is using preference and perception to convince buyers to purchase their clothes. It originated in the 1930s, and is predominate in the UK and America.

Imagine shopping in a department store or boutique and picking up a shirt that is labeled as a medium. You try it on and its waaaay too big on you. You try a small and it fits much better. But you're typically a medium? This makes you feel more confident and definetly makes you feel good about yourself. Since you're feeling good about yourself, you decide to buy it. You also buy shorts to go with it. This because of vanity sizing.

How Vanity Sizing Affects Profits

Vanity sizing is believed to produce billions of dollars in sales every year.

The Affects of Size Inconsistency

Due to inconsistent sizing, items purchased are often returned because of varying label sizes. Across different brands and companies, sizes vary greatly. As many business are going online, consumers are having a difficult time finding the correct sized item. Vanity sizing didnt pose a huge problem when people shopped in brick and mortar shops, when they would try on articles of clothing before they purchased it. As online shopping grows and flourishes, it becomes more and more difficult to find an appropriate size.
